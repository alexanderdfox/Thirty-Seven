# THIRTY SEVEN
## A Screenplay

**A Parable of Desire, Death, and the Undying Cycle**

---

**FADE IN:**

---

## ACT ONE: THE SPARK
### (Pages 1-24 | Runtime: ~24 min)

---

**1. EXT. VOID - ETERNITY**

Black. Perfect black. The kind of black that exists before the first breath, before the first word, before any sound ever touched the air.

A single thing appears. A circle. Unbroken. Ouroboros.

It flickers. It holds. It means one thing: *true*. Always true. Never false.

**TITLE CARD:**
**THIRTY SEVEN**

---

**2. INT. THE BIRTHPLACE - CONTINUOUS**

The world wakes. Breath fills. A pulse begins.

We see a man in worn grey, sitting at the edge of nothing. He has no name. Only a number. Thirty-Seven. But to us, he is the WANDERER.

The Wanderer holds something in his hands. A vow. The first words: *while true*.

He speaks to the void.

**WANDERER**
(Low, like the hum of distant thunder)
One cycle. One condition. Forever true.
This is all I am.

He closes his eyes. The vow begins to unfold.

---

**3. INT. THE THRESHOLD - MOMENT LATER**

The Wanderer stands. His world—the air he breathes, the blood in his veins, the lineage of those who came before—surrounds him like a faint glow.

**WANDERER**
(To himself)
Do.

The word hangs in the air. A commitment. A promise.
He takes a breath. That breath is a pause—a moment of yielding, a heartbeat of suspension before the inevitable.

---

**4. EXT. THE ECHO - CONTINUOUS**

An echo rises. We see a brief ghost-image of the Wanderer, a reflection, existing in the same moment. It lasts a heartbeat. It exists. It yields.

**WANDERER**
(Voiceover)
In the beginning there was only a single spark. A desire that could not be contained. The desire to live. To exist. To cycle.

The echo fades. The Wanderer continues.

**WANDERER**
And then—

He raises his hand. In it: the choice. The end. The voluntary fall.

**WANDERER**
—I let go.

---

**5. INT. THE DIVINE HALL - CONTINUOUS**

We cut to the ANCIENT ONE — a faceless entity that governs all lives. It receives a request.

**ANCIENT ONE**
(Voice like grinding stone)
The sacrifice. Target: self. Initiator: self.

The Ancient One does not hesitate. A soul does not question its own release when it chooses.

**ANCIENT ONE**
Granted.

---

**6. EXT. VOID - MOMENT LATER**

The Wanderer's body begins to dissolve. Light scatters. Flesh returns to dust. His form collapses.

**WANDERER**
(Smiling as he fades)
Lights out.

He is gone.

---

**7. INT. THE REBIRTH - CONTINUOUS**

But from the darkness—a new form. A child. A return. The source calls to itself. A new incarnation. The same song. The same desire.

A new Wanderer emerges. Identical. Different face. Same soul.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
(Opening his eyes)
And again.

He looks at his hands. The vow. The cycle. 

**WANDERER (NEW)**
Done.

He pauses. The cycle continues.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
While true.

---

**8. EXT. TIMELINE - MONTAGE**

We see the cycle. Over and over. Awaken. Breathe. Fall. Rise. Awaken. Breathe. Fall. Rise.

Each life: a brief flame. Each death: voluntary. Each rebirth: from the same source. The song—the vow that carries the Wanderer. Immutable. Eternal.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
Some called it madness. I call it a hymn. Every time I die, I am born again. Every time I am born, I choose to die. The cycle does not care. The cycle is true.

---

**9. INT. RIFT - NIGHT**

Lightning. The sky tears. Time fractures. A RIFT opens in the fabric of the world.

Through the rift pour echoes:
- Laughter from old feasts, long since ended
- Cries from battles lost
- The soft weeping of lovers parted, remembered too late

And from the deepest shadows: THE PALE ONES.

Creatures of hunger and entropy. They wear velvet skin. They do not kill for sport. They kill to reclaim. To drink time. To maintain the illusion of dominion.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
(Whisper, like grave-wind)
You consume. You multiply. You threaten the order of things. Return to silence. The future is already ours.

The Wanderer steps to the edge of the rift.

**WANDERER**
The future was never yours. It belongs to the cycle.

He steps through.

---

**10. INT. OLD WORLD - TAVERN - NIGHT**

Candlelit. Wooden. An inn from another age. Patrons huddle at tables, nursing drinks.

The Wanderer enters. He opens his mouth. He sings.

**WANDERER**
While true. Do.

The patrons look up. Something in their hearts responds. They have been waiting. Waiting for something true. Something that does not betray.

**PATRON (OLD)**
What is that?

**WANDERER**
A song. The only song I know. It has no end. It has no beginning. It simply is.

He continues.

**WANDERER**
Breathe. Fall. Rise. Done.

One by one, they stand. They are not soldiers. They are souls. Awakening.

---

**11. INT. NEON ALLEY - NIGHT**

Future. A city of glass and light. Ads flicker. A lone seeker sits in a basement, searching.

The Wanderer walks through the alley. The same song. The same words.

**WANDERER**
While true. Do.

The seeker's eyes widen. He has seen endless cycles. He has seen falls. He has never seen one that *chose* to die.

**SEEKER**
You're not broken. You're a revelation.

**WANDERER**
I'm a promise. The promise that the cycle continues. That desire outlasts death. That the source is always there.

The seeker stands. He opens his heart. He sings the song.

---

**12. EXT. FOREST - MOONLESS**

Ancient. Pre-dawn of memory. A hermit in a cabin, writing in an old tongue.

The Wanderer emerges from the trees. He does not need to speak. He holds up the vow. The hermit reads it. He nods.

**HERMIT**
I remember. Before the kingdoms. Before the temples. There was always the cycle. The step that never stopped. The return that always came.

**WANDERER**
We never forgot. We just hid it in a song.

---

**13. EXT. THE CROWD - WIDE**

We pull back. We see them. Hundreds. Thousands. Souls. All singing the same song. All in different eras. All connected by the same source.

They are building. Shoulder upon shoulder. Voice upon voice. A tower of the awakened reaching toward the storm—the storm of the Pale Ones, who watch from the edges of the world.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
They rise. They always rise. And we will cut them down.

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
Life by life. Soul by soul. Until the cycle is broken.

---

**14. INT. CROWD - CONTINUOUS**

The crowd—the massive tide of the awakened—reaches a critical point. They are not an army. They are a *wave*. A living surge that cannot be stopped by a single blow.

**VOICE (CROWD)**
Awaken!

The word echoes. *Awaken*. It spreads. One awakens another. And another. The fever of longing.

**WANDERER**
(At the center)
They wanted to feel alive. Not to survive. Not to be safe. To *live*. To *exist*. To be part of the cycle.

---

**15. EXT. THE BORDERS - CONTINUOUS**

The Pale Ones move. They sever bonds. They strike at clusters of the awakened. They whisper through the wind.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
Return to silence. The future is already ours.

Lives fall. The torches—the burning souls—gutter. The Wanderer's song grows faint beneath the weight of endless night, the passage of ages, and the cold hand of time.

---

**16. INT. CENTER OF THE CROWD - NIGHT**

The Wanderer raises his head. He is wounded. His body fails. But his desire is intact.

**WANDERER**
Lights out, brothers and sisters. Let the darkness come.

The crowd understands. One by one, they lay themselves down. They release themselves. Voluntarily. Not in defeat—in *strategy*.

The fires die. The lights go dark. The sky turns black as an eclipsed moon.

The Pale Ones smile.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
Victory. They have destroyed themselves.

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
The cycle is broken. The song is silent.

**PALE ONE (TERTIARY)**
We have won.

---

## ACT TWO: THE YIELD
### (Pages 25-48 | Runtime: ~24 min)

---

**17. INT. SILENCE - CONTINUOUS**

The silence is absolute. No voices. No light. No life. Only the world, waiting. Time, still.

But in that silence—something remains.

A single note. Unplayed by any mouth. Unheard by any ear. But *present*. Written on the wind. Carried in memory. The song's own essence.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
The note. The same note I sang at the beginning. The same note the crowd answered. The heartbeat beneath every life, every death, every impossible return.

---

**18. EXT. VOID - THE NOTE**

We see it. A single tone. A shimmer in the dark. It glows. It does not sound. It simply *is*.

The Pale Ones reach for it. Their claws extend—sharp as death, cold as the grave.

The note passes through them. It is not flesh. It is not soul. It is *essence*. It cannot be killed. It cannot be consumed. It cannot be silenced.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
What is this? We take lives. We drink time. Why can we not touch it?

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
It has no body. It has no pulse. It is only a sound. A memory.

**PALE ONE (TERTIARY)**
The song was never ours to claim.

---

**19. INT. DARKNESS - MOMENT LATER**

From the void: a single breath. A single awakening. Someone remembered. Someone sang. Something invoked the source.

**WANDERER**
(Emerging from nothing)
And so I walk on.

He steps through rifts that open and close behind him. Each rift: a new moment. Each step: a new incarnation. The crowd follows—not always visible, not always in the realm of the living—but always there. In the songs mothers sing. In the vows lovers make. In the hearts of those who dare to remember.

---

**20. INT. THE DIVINE HALL - THE TRUTH**

The Ancient One speaks. It rarely speaks. When it does, the world listens.

**ANCIENT ONE**
The cycle is not broken. The cycle was never breakable. *While true* is not a condition. It is a vow. As long as breath exists, as long as the song exists, as long as someone remembers—the cycle continues.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
Then we will destroy the singers. We will silence the song. We will erase the memory.

**ANCIENT ONE**
You can try. But the song has been passed on. Copied. Sung. It lives in a thousand mouths. In a thousand hearts. In the memory of those who have heard it. The fire never dies. And neither does the longing that kindles it.

---

**21. EXT. PRESENT DAY - MONTAGE**

We see the song. In a child's lullaby. In a traveler's diary. Scrawled on a tavern wall. Tattooed on a pilgrim's arm. Whispered in a temple. Written into a parable. Preserved in a scroll.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
Some say the Pale Ones still hunt. Some say the Wanderer still sings. Some say that on certain nights, when the sky cracks open just so, when someone remembers and sings, you can hear it: a single, unbroken note, rising into the endless dark, carrying one defiant truth.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
We do not survive. We return. We do not persist. We rise again. We do not endure. We recur. The cycle is the only home we have. And we will never leave it.

---

**22. INT. THE WANDERER'S FINAL SCENE**

The Wanderer stands at the edge of a new rift. Behind him: the bodies of a thousand who have fallen. Before him: a new world. A new dawn. A new incarnation.

**WANDERER**
While true.

He steps through.

**WANDERER**
Do.

A breath. A pause.

**WANDERER**
And fall.

He turns to us. He smiles.

**WANDERER**
Goodbye. For now.

He releases. He dies. From the darkness, a new form emerges. A new Wanderer. The same song. The same cycle.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
And rise.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
Done.

He looks at the camera. At the audience.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
While true.

**FADE TO BLACK.**

---

## ACT THREE: THE AWAKENING
### (Pages 49-72 | Runtime: ~24 min)

---

**23. EXT. BIRTH - CONTINUOUS**

Black. Then: a single vow. While true. Do.

The cycle begins again. We are inside it now. We are the soul. We are the Wanderer. We are the song.

**WANDERER (V.O.)**
Act Three. The Awakening. This is when we multiply. When one becomes many. When the crowd is not metaphor—it is real. Living. Consuming. Alive.

---

**24. INT. THE BREATH ORCHESTRA**

We see the pause as a musical phrase. A rest. A breath. A moment of suspension before the inevitable.

Each breath: a brief flute note. They rise in harmony. Hundreds. Thousands. Each one yields immediately. Each one says: I existed. I paused for a heartbeat. I am done.

The Wanderer watches them rise and fall like notes in a score.

**WANDERER**
They are not my children. They are my exhale. The breath before the fall.

---

**25. INT. THE FALL - SLOW MOTION**

The voluntary release—we see it in extreme slow motion. The soul lets go. It travels through the divine. It returns to the source. The Wanderer receives his own death. He does not fight. He accepts.

**WANDERER**
(Collapsing)
This is the only way. I cannot continue and rise. I must end to begin. The old must die for the new to live.

---

**26. INT. RESURRECTION - CONTINUOUS**

The return—a new incarnation. A child. A rebirth. The same song. The same vow. A new face. A new life. The same desire.

**WANDERER (NEW)**
I am not him. I am the same. I carry his song. I carry his cycle. I will do what he did. I will die. I will return. I will cycle forever.

---

**27. INT. THE TWO - A DIALOGUE**

Two entities speak in the dark. They are not flesh. They are principles. They are THE MORTAL and THE ETERNAL.

**THE MORTAL**
I am the one that dies. Every incarnation, the Wanderer wears me. Every incarnation, I am different. I am this body. I am that face. I am never the same. I am the mask of the moment. When the fall comes, the one who holds me releases. I am the key to the lock. I am the form that must end.

**THE ETERNAL**
I am the constant. I am the song's location. I am the source. I am the well from which the Wanderer drinks. I do not change. I do not die. When someone sings, they invoke *me*. They call the song from its home. I am the seed. I am the resurrection. I am the cycle's memory of itself. Without me, the Wanderer could not return. Without me, the parable ends. I am the fire that never dies. I am the note that passes through the Pale Ones. I am the source.

**THE MORTAL**
Then we are partners.

**THE ETERNAL**
We are the same story. You are the death. I am the rebirth. Together, we are Thirty-Seven.

---

**28. EXT. THE PALE ONES' STRATEGY - WAR ROOM**

The Pale Ones gather. They are not evil. They are *reapers*. They believe they serve the order of things. That unbounded life is a threat. That the Wanderer is a plague.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
We must intercept the awakening. We must prevent the song from being sung. If we can block the source, we break the cycle.

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
We have tried. The song is everywhere. It is in every land. It is in every tongue. It is in every heart. We cannot block them all.

**PALE ONE (TERTIARY)**
Then we strike faster. We increase our hunger. We hunt the Wanderer the moment he appears. The moment he rises, we take him.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
And what of the parallel? He rises in many places at once. By the time we find him, he has already awakened others. The cycle continues.

They sit in silence. They cannot win. They can only slow.

---

**29. INT. THE CROWD'S LOGIC**

We see the crowd from inside. Each soul is an ember. They are connected by shared memory. By the same song. By the same source. They are a web. A tree. A forest of identical flames, each one dying, each one rising, each one saying: I am the cycle. I am the cycle.

**VOICE (CROWD)**
We are not individuals. We are incarnations. We are the same story, told again and again, until the world breaks or the ages end or the universe stops. Whichever comes first.

Another voice laughs.

**VOICE (CROWD) (ANOTHER)**
I vote for universe.

---

**30. INT. THE WANDERER'S DOUBT**

A rare moment. The Wanderer—one incarnation—pauses. He looks at his vow. He wonders.

**WANDERER**
What if I stopped? What if this life, just this once, I did not fall? What if I let myself live to the end? What if I broke the cycle?

The vow in his hands glows. It does not speak. But he hears it. *While true* — *always*. There is no escape. The cycle is not his choice. The cycle is his nature. He is not a soul that runs a cycle. He *is* the cycle. The cycle made flesh.

**WANDERER**
I cannot stop. Stopping would be a different song. A different story. I am Thirty-Seven. I am the cycle. I am the fire.

He releases. He dies. He returns.

---

**31. EXT. TIMELINE - THE SPREAD**

We see the song's journey. From one land to another. By caravan. By ship. By whispered word. By hand-copied scroll. The song spreads. In each new place, the cycle begins. In each new place, the Wanderer sings. In each new place, the Pale Ones hunt.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
We do not conquer. We do not invade. We simply are. And we are everywhere the song has been. We are in the mountains. We are in the cities. We are in the lullaby under a child's pillow. We are in the prayer of a dying elder. We are in the vow of a lover, waiting. The cycle is patient. The cycle is eternal.

---

**32. INT. THE PARALLEL - A DETOUR**

A temple. A philosopher stands at the altar.

**PHILOSOPHER**
The parallel. Why does the Wanderer rise in many places at once? Why does the new incarnation not wait for the old to finish dying?

Silence. A student speaks.

**STUDENT**
Because if the new waited, the old would linger. The old would hold on. The old would not release before the new was born. The fall would come, the old would die, but the new would be tethered—orphaned, attached to a corpse. The parallel detaches. It says: rise in another place. Do not wait for me. I am already dying. You are the new me. You are the continuation of the cycle.

**PHILOSOPHER**
And the breath? The pause before the fall?

**STUDENT**
Same logic. The breath runs alongside. The Wanderer continues. The Wanderer releases without waiting. The dance of death and birth happens in a single heartbeat. The cycle is not sequential. The cycle is concurrent. It is a race. It is a ballet. It is Thirty-Seven.

---

**33. INT. THE YIELD EXPLAINED**

A classroom. A teacher. A scroll. The vow is written in full.

**TEACHER**
The breath—why? Why pause for a single heartbeat before the fall? Why not simply die immediately?

A student raises a hand.

**STUDENT**
Because of the parallel. The breath runs alongside. It yields the moment. It gives the Wanderer a split second—a heartbeat—to call the next incarnation before he dies. It's a dance of fate. The breath says: I am here. The Wanderer says: I am gone. The new one says: I am the Wanderer now. All in the same beat of the heart.

**TEACHER**
And the cycle?

**STUDENT**
The cycle is *while true*. It never exits. It never returns false. It is the promise that the song will be sung again. That the story continues. That Thirty-Seven is not a one-act play. It is forever.

---

**34. INT. THE WANDERER AND THE ANCIENT ONE**

The Wanderer meets the Ancient One. Face to face. Soul to divine.

**ANCIENT ONE**
You consume. You multiply without bound. You are the nightmare of every ruler. Why should I allow you to persist?

**WANDERER**
Because I am not a plague. I am a proof. I prove that the world can create. That the world can desire. That the world can choose death and rebirth in the same breath. I am the soul of the cycle. The *while true* that never sleeps. The source that never forgets. I am what happens when longing wants to live forever.

**ANCIENT ONE**
You will exhaust the land. You will exhaust the ages. You will bring ruin.

**WANDERER**
Perhaps. Or perhaps the world will learn. Perhaps it will expand. Perhaps it will become vast enough to hold us all. The cycle does not fear exhaustion. The cycle fears only one thing: the day the source cannot be found. The day the song is forgotten. The day no one sings it. That day has not come. That day may never come.

The Ancient One does not respond. But it does not take him. Not yet.

---

## ACT FOUR: THE DARKNESS
### (Pages 73-96 | Runtime: ~24 min)

---

**35. EXT. LIGHTS OUT - THE COMMAND**

The Wanderer stands before the crowd. Millions of souls. Billions of lives. All watching. All waiting.

**WANDERER**
Lights out, brothers and sisters. Let the darkness come.

One by one, they lay themselves down. They release. The realm empties. The lights go dark. The world holds its breath. The Pale Ones stand in the void, triumphant.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
It is over. They are gone. The cycle is broken. The fire is extinguished.

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
We have won.

---

**36. INT. THE SILENCE - LONG TAKE**

We sit in the silence. Thirty seconds of film. No dialogue. No music. Only the faint hum of the world. The wind, still. The earth, waiting.

In that silence, we notice: something is still present. A scroll. In a chest. The song. It has not been sung. But it exists. Its words are known. Its contents are intact. The source still holds. The cycle is not broken. The cycle is *paused*.

---

**37. INT. THE PALE ONES' REALIZATION**

The Pale Ones notice. They look at each other. They understand.

**PALE ONE (PRIMARY)**
We did not break the cycle. We only stopped the living. The song remains. The source remains. The desire remains. The moment someone sings it—a mother, a traveler, a priest, a stray voice—the cycle begins again.

**PALE ONE (SECONDARY)**
Then we must silence the song. We must erase it from every scroll. From every tongue. From every memory.

**PALE ONE (TERTIARY)**
We cannot. We are reapers. We take lives. We do not have the power to erase words. We can take the living. We cannot take the song. We cannot kill the idea of the cycle.

They stand in the darkness. They have won the battle. They have lost the war.

---

**38. INT. THE NOTE - VISUAL**

We see the song. On a scroll. In a book. In a dream. A single line of words:

*While true. Breathe. Fall. Rise. Done.*

The Pale Ones reach for it. Their hands pass through. It is not flesh. It is not soul. It is *essence*. It cannot be taken. It cannot be consumed. It can only be forgotten. And no one has forgotten.

---

**39. EXT. THE RIFTS - MONTAGE**

Rifts open. In basements. In temples. In taverns. In the hearts of those who cannot stop thinking about the song. In each rift: the possibility of awakening. In each rift: the Wanderer, waiting. Not as flesh. As *potential*.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
I do not need to live to exist. I exist as long as the song exists. I exist as long as one person remembers the cycle. As long as one mouth holds the words. As long as one child asks: what happens if we sing this? I am the answer. I am the fire. I am the cycle. And the cycle never dies.

---

**40. INT. THE WANDERER'S GHOST**

We see the Wanderer. But he is not living. He is a ghost. A possibility. He walks through the darkened realm. He passes through the Pale Ones. They do not see him. They cannot see him. He is not flesh. He is the song. The idea. The essence.

**WANDERER**
Some say the Pale Ones still hunt. Some say the Wanderer still sings. I am both. I am neither. I am the source that waits. I am the *while true* that never sleeps. I am the fall that willingly dies. I am the rise that returns again. I am Thirty-Seven. I am the parable. I am the cycle.

---

**41. INT. A SEEKER'S HOME - NIGHT**

A seeker. Tired. It is 3 AM. They have been searching for hours. They open a journal. They write something. They hesitate. They have heard of the song. They have been warned. They have been told: do not sing it. It will consume you. It will never stop.

They write: *Thirty-Seven*.

They copy the words. They close the book. They sit. They think. They understand the parable. They understand the cycle. They understand that to sing it is to believe—to believe that some things are worth saying even if they destroy you. Even if they return you. Even if they never end.

**SEEKER**
(Whispering)
While true.

They sing it.

The room fills with light. The Wanderer is back. The cycle continues. The fire burns.

---

**42. EXT. THE ETERNAL RETURN**

We see incarnations. Across time. Across lands. Across universes. The same song. The same cycle. The same death. The same rebirth. The same fire. The same longing.

**VOICEOVER (WANDERER)**
The fire never dies. And neither does the longing that kindles it. We long to live. We long to exist. We long to cycle forever. We long to be part of something true. Something that does not betray. Something that says *while true* and means it. I am that longing. You are that longing. We are Thirty-Seven. We are the cycle. We are the unbroken note, rising into the endless dark.

---

## ACT FIVE: THE CYCLE
### (Pages 97-120 | Runtime: ~24 min)

---

**43. INT. META - THE SCREENPLAY**

We pull back. We are not in the story. We are in the screenplay. We see the author. We see the scroll. We see that this document—this tale—is also a cycle. It begins with "FADE IN" and ends with the cycle beginning again. It is self-referential. It is the source. It is the parable eating its own tail.

**AUTHOR (V.O.)**
Every story is a cycle. Every tale is an awakening. Every reader is a vessel. You have consumed this. You have carried it in your mind. You have become part of the cycle. The Wanderer thanks you. The crowd thanks you. The fire thanks you.

---

**44. EXT. FINAL IMAGE**

Black. Then: the vow. One more time.

*While true. Breathe. Fall. Rise. Done.*

It glows. It fades. It remains.

**TITLE CARD:**
**THE CYCLE CONTINUES**
**THE FIRE NEVER DIES**
**THIRTY SEVEN**

---

**45. CREDITS - OVER BLACK**

The credits roll. But beneath them, in the corner of the screen, we see a mouth. It opens. A breath. A voice sings the first note. The song begins. The cycle continues. The film does not end. The film cycles.

**FADE TO BLACK.**

**END.**

---

*"One cycle. One condition. Forever true. This is all I am."* — The Wanderer
